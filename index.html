Good Shepherd Microfinance Case Competition

The GMCC provides students with an insight into the world of microfinance and consulting. The case competition was on a live project named LauchME, a low-interest small loan scheme for low-income care workers who want to start a business.
The role involved determining the location of a new base of operations for the program. Using ABS census data I create an interactive map (click here) of the target audience's location. In addition, I created a product development strategy to increase funding, which we call the Bank and Established Business Incentive Scheme. See the slides to the right for more information.
Skills, such as communication, leadership, and creativity were all utilized in the process in creating this case.

Please note the video of the interactive map was uploaded to a teammate's account, Connor Price, though it is my work.

Data Investigation: How to earn over $50K annually in the US

A personal project of mine which investigates the driving factors in predict where or not an individual earns above $50,000 annually in the US. The project determines that your captial gain or positive returns on investments, being married or in a civil parntership and individual's number of years in education are the main factors in determining income over and above $50,000 annually. Though there are some interesting interactions between capital losses and age. Click the title of this section for more information.
The project makes use of a XGBoost classifier to construct the model, Permutation Importatance lists, and SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values and summay plots to determine how each feature effects the model's prediction.

Model's SHAP Summary Plot

This plot shows each data point for every feature in our model, and its respective effects (horizontal position) on the model's prediction of income above and below $50K a year.
